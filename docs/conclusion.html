Identifying Holidaymaking Behaviours in Leeds through Spatial Microsimulation

Chapter 5 Conclusion

This study attempted to investigate the profile of those holiday makers to city destinations in the USA. As a normal problem faced by microsimulation, oversampling occurred in some parts of the city center and its surroundings. In the data and methods section, the different destinations according to holiday type were demonstrated. In the results section, together with the five variables, age band, household income, number of children, and satisfaction with most recent holiday and Supergroup name, we developed a unique consumer profile. In conclusion, it is suggested that the targeted market be developed around the young professionals aged between 35 to 49 who have children, and a relatively high income of 41-50k.
